I was selected as one of the **ten PhD finalists from across the University of Bologna** for the 2026 Three-Minute-Thesis Competition.

In **“Artificial Hive Minds”**, I presented the central idea of my doctoral research to a general audience in less than three minutes and with the support of a single slide. The talk explained how distributed artificial agents can cooperate, learn from local information, and collectively adapt without depending on a single centralized intelligence.

The competition was a challenging and rewarding exercise in communicating complex research in a concise, accessible, and engaging way.
